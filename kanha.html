Shyam's Courtyard

𝄞

Not a sermon. Not a lecture. Just a courtyard where you can sit, breathe, and let something true find you. All 18 chapters of the Gita. Every verse in my own words.

Jump to a chapter
Chapter 2 · Sankhya Yoga

Nainam chindanti shastrani, nainam dahati pavakah

"The soul cannot be cut by weapons, nor burned by fire."

Whatever they've done to you. However many times you've been broken apart by something that felt final. That part of you that makes you you is still whole. Still here. Still yours. The things that tried to reduce you couldn't reach the actual you. That's not a small thing. That's the whole thing.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 3 · Karma Yoga

Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana

"You have the right to work, but never to its fruits."

Do the thing. Put in the work. Write the chapter, have the conversation, take the step. Then let go of needing it to turn out a certain way. That's not passivity. That's not giving up. That's actually the most difficult kind of faith there is. The kind where you act fully and then release the outcome completely. Most people can't do it. He's saying try anyway.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 4 · Jnana Yoga

Yada yada hi dharmasya glanir bhavati bharata

"Whenever dharma declines, I arise."

He shows up when things fall apart. Not always in the way you're expecting. Not always as something you recognize. Sometimes as a stranger who says exactly the right thing at the exact wrong moment. Sometimes as a book that falls open to the page you needed. Sometimes just as a very specific kind of quiet that arrives when you're sitting with something difficult. He keeps showing up. He has said so himself.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 6 · Dhyana Yoga

Uddharet atmana atmanam

"Let a person lift themselves by their own self."

You are both the problem and the solution. That sounds harsh but it's actually the most liberating thing I've ever read. Because it means the thing that can fix this is already inside you. Not waiting to arrive. Already there. You just keep forgetting it's yours. He's reminding you. Lift yourself. You have the hands for it.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 9 · Raja Vidya Yoga

Ananyash chintayanto mam ye janah paryupasate

"Those who think of me alone — I carry what they need."

He doesn't need you to be perfect before you can come to him. He doesn't need you to have figured everything out first. Even a quiet "I don't know how to do this anymore" is enough. Even that counts as turning toward him. That's a very low bar. I think he set it low on purpose. So nobody would have an excuse not to reach.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 11 · Vishwarupa Darshana Yoga

Drshtvedam manushyam rupam tava saumyam janardana

"Having seen your universal form, I am comforted by your gentle human face."

Arjuna saw the entire cosmos inside Kanha. The infinite. The terrifying and the beautiful all at once. And then he asked to see the familiar face again. The gentle one. The one he recognized. I think about this a lot. Even when you have access to the infinite, sometimes what you need is the presence. The specific face. The person who is just there. He gave that back too. Without judgment. He understood.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 12 · Bhakti Yoga

Advesta sarva bhutanam maitrah karuna eva cha

"One who hates no being, who is friendly and compassionate to all."

The whole chapter is basically a description of someone who is genuinely good. Not good in a performance. Not good when people are watching. Actually, quietly, consistently good at the level of how they move through the world. That's the person he's describing as dear to him. Not the most knowledgeable. Not the most disciplined. The most genuinely kind. That's the goal. I think about this whenever I'm tempted to make something more complicated than it is.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 15 · Purushottama Yoga

Tam eva chaadyam purusham prapadye

"I take refuge in that primal being."

Short. Three words in meaning. No explanation attached. Sometimes the most honest thing you can say is just that. I don't understand all of this. I can't fix it from where I'm standing. I'm going to rest in something older and steadier than whatever is currently falling apart around me. That's it. That's the whole verse. Sometimes that's all you need.

🪷
🪈
Chapter 18 · Moksha Yoga · The Last Word

Sarvadharman parityajya mam ekam sharanam vraja

"Abandon all duties and take shelter in me alone. I will free you from all sins. Do not grieve."

The last chapter. The final word after 17 chapters of philosophy and instruction and cosmic revelation. And what he says at the end, after everything, is essentially: come here. I've got it. Don't worry. Not a command. A promise. Do not grieve. That's the most tender thing anyone has said at the end of a very long conversation. After all that, after all the verses and the teachings and the vision of the infinite, the last thing he wanted Arjuna to leave with was the absence of grief. I think about this every time something feels too big. He already said it. Do not grieve.
